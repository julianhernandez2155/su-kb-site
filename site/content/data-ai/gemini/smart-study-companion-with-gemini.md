Transform how you learn with a personalized Study Gem. Upload class materials and let Gemini explain difficult concepts, quiz you on topics, and create custom study guides  all from your SU Google account.

# Create a Personalized Tutor Gem

**Youll need:**Course materials (PDFs, slides, lecture notes, or study guides)

#### Steps:

1. From the left sidebar, select **Explore Gems** to browse or create your own AI helper.
2. Click **+ New Gem.** Give it a descriptive name like *Smart Study Companion* or *BIO 121 Tutor*.
3. Add instructions in the description and instruction fields to guide your Study Gem.

   - *Example:*You are my personal study tutor. Explain concepts in simple terms, create 5-question quizzes, and summarize uploaded lecture slides.
4. Upload your lecture notes, slides, or readings. Then ask:

   - Create a quiz for Chapter 2, or Explain the main themes from this document.

---
💬 *Example prompts:*

> [!note]- Generate 5 multiple-choice questions from these notes.
> Generate **5 multiple-choice questions** based on this document or lecture content.
>
> For each question:
>
> - Provide **4 answer options** (AD).
> - Indicate the **correct answer** and include a **one-sentence explanation**.
> - Mix conceptual, definition-based, and applied questions.
> - Keep the questions at a college level (not overly simple).
>
> At the end, include a short summary titled **Concepts Reviewed** listing the key topics covered in the quiz.

> [!note]- Summarize this article in 3 main points.
> Summarize this article into **3 main points** that capture the key arguments or findings.
>
> For each main point:
>
> - Provide a **23 sentence explanation**.
> - Highlight any significant data, evidence, or examples that support it.
>
> Conclude with a short **Overall Insight** section (2 sentences) that explains how these points connect or why they matter in context.

> [!note]- Explain this formula step by step like a tutor.
> Explain this formula **step by step** as if you were tutoring a student:
>
> 1. **Name and Purpose:** Briefly describe what the formula is used for.
> 2. **Define Variables:** List and define each variable or symbol in plain language.
> 3. **Step-by-Step Breakdown:** Explain how to apply the formula, showing an example calculation if possible.
> 4. **Common Mistakes:** Identify typical errors students make when using this formula.
> 5. **Check for Understanding:** End with one short practice question the student can solve.
>
> Keep explanations clear, structured, and easy to follow.

> [!note]- Create a flashcard set for this topic.
> Create a **flashcard set** based on this topic or document.
>
> Each card should have:
>
> - **Front:** A key term, concept, or question.
> - **Back:** A concise definition or 12 sentence explanation.
>
> Generate at least **10 flashcards** that cover the main ideas, terms, and examples.
>
> Format them as:
> Front: [Term/Question]
> Back: [Definition/Answer]
>
> End the set with a short **Review Tip** suggesting how to use the cards for effective studying.

> [!info]
> ### **Tips for using Tutor Gem**
>
> - Be specific  tell Gemini what level of detail you need (college-level summary, 2-minute explanation, etc.).
> - Reuse your Study Gem before every exam to keep your learning consistent.
> - Edit or update your Gem anytime as you add new course content.
> - Save and share a general Study Guide Gem link with classmates (see below).

## FAQ  Limitations

> [!note]- When I share a New Gem, do other users get access to my personal data or materials?
> When you share a Gem link, others can view or use your *Gems setup* (its name, instructions, and structure), but they do **not** have access to your uploaded files, past chats, or custom content. Each users data remains private under their own SU Google account.

> [!note]- How do I fill out the criteria for a new Gem?
> Each Gem includes sections for **Name**, **Description**, **Instructions**, and **Knowledge** (uploaded files). For help writing effective instructions, refer to:
> 👉 <https://support.google.com/gemini/answer/15235603?hl=en>

> [!note]- What kinds of files can I upload to my Gem?
> You can upload common study file types like **.pdf, .docx, .txt, .pptx, or Google Docs** directly from your Drive. Avoid uploading restricted or confidential data such as graded assignments, exams, or student records.

### **Keep in Mind**

- Avoid uploading confidential or graded materials.
- Review AI-generated quizzes for accuracy.
- Always double-check facts  Gemini is a learning support tool, not a replacement for understanding.
